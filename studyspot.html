Project Overview

StudySpot was created as a student-centered application concept that helps users locate the best on-campus study spaces through visual cues, map interaction, and quick-access information. This prototype was created to satisfy a group project assignment for a UI/UX course. The entire idea was conceived between my group members and I, while I lead on the construction and design elements of the project. The overall goal here was to make finding an appropriate study environment faster, easier, and more intuitive.

What I Worked On:

My contributions to the project focused on development of the prototype interface and building out the look and feel for the user. This also includes key screens and interactions that support the user journey while navigating the prototype. My Contributed Screens

Homepage Experience

The homepage establishes the app’s core purpose by combining search, map visibility, and quick study filters into a single landing experience that helps users start fast.

Location Detail Pages

These pages give students a deeper look at a specific location, including noise level, seating, map positioning, and useful amenities that help them decide where to go. I created 12 individual focused pages that relay all of this information.

Interactive Map View

The map screen helps users visually compare locations across campus, using color-coded indicators and quick info to support faster decision-making among students.
