My Journey:

Before transferring to UCF, I focused more on starting my IT career while working toward my A.A. I decided to learn in the way I knew best, and threw myself into hands on IT roles. I quickly landed myself into roles that built up my personal foundation, becoming a Tech Support Specialist and eventually moving elsewhere to work as a Remote Hardware Technician.

After obtaining my A.A, I realized my purpose in exploring the IT landscape is to learn as much as possible, and leverage that knowledge to be applied to my future career path. My need for learning and personal development led me to my current job, where I've held the paid position as an IT Product Management Intern at my current company for 8 months now. It was a new field and I was excited to dive in head first! I turned the initial 3 month internship into two separate extensions, and have been given an offer for full time employment following graduation!
