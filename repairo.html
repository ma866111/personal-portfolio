Featured Project

Repairo
Project Management & Scope Planning Showcase.

Repairo is an Instrument Repair Management & Tracking System created for Lake Baldwin Music Academy. In this project for a Project Management course, I served as the Project Manager and helped shape the planning, scope structure, and documentation strategy for the system. This page presents the project as a leadership and documentation case study rather than a traditional application walkthrough.

Project Management Scope Planning Documentation Team Leadership
Project: My role was to be the acting Project Manager for Harmonic Solutions.
Focus: Scope management, structured planning, process control, and deliverable organization.

Project Overview

Repairo was proposed as a digital software solution to move Lake Baldwin Music Academy away from its paper-based instrument repair process and into a more structured system for repair requests, inventory handling, tracking, and reporting.

Leadership Contribution:

In this project, I served as the acting Project Manager and helped organize the planning structure, scope documentation, and change-control logic that made the project understandable and manageable from a leadership perspective. I also created the key planning diagrams for the project, instructed my team on how the format of the documentation template should look, and delegated work.

Current Presentation:

This project demonstrates that my work is not limited to technical builds alone. It also shows my ability to lead a team, organize documentation, define scope, and present project structure in a professional manner in a decision-ready format.

MOV & Success Criteria

The MOV, or the Measurable Organizational Value, defines success in terms of our respective organization. Our metrics were established in terms of repair efficiency, customer satisfaction, operational cost reduction, and system accessibility.

Repair Cycle Goal Reduce average repair time from 14 days to 5–7 days.
Customer Satisfaction Target 95% or greater satisfaction for repair services.
Cost Reduction Decrease repair operational costs by at least 25%.
System Accessibility Maintain 99.5% uptime during business operating hours.

Use Case Diagram

Scope Change Process

One of the strongest management elements in this project is the clearly documented scope-change process, which lays out how changes are requested, reviewed, approved, and implemented.

Structured Change Control:

The process begins with a formal change request, followed by preliminary and technical impact assessments, then review by a change control board before implementation planning and monitoring.

Documentation Templates:

The plan also includes templates for a Scope Change Request Form (SCR-1), Initial Impact Assessment (SCR-2), and Detailed Impact Analysis Report (SCR-3), reinforcing a disciplined and repeatable process.
